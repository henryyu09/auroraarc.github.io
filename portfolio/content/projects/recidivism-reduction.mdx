## Summary

A comprehensive statistical analysis of recidivism patterns in Iowa's prison system, identifying key risk factors and evaluating the effectiveness of intervention programs. Published in the Society of Actuaries ARCH 2023 proceedings.

## Technical Approach

Using longitudinal correctional data, we applied survival analysis (Cox proportional hazards models) and random forest classifiers to identify the most significant predictors of re-offense within 3 years of release.

<Callout type="warning">
Working with correctional data requires careful attention to bias — historical policing patterns can confound recidivism metrics.
</Callout>

## Results

Key findings:
- Educational programs reduced recidivism risk by approximately 22%
- Employment assistance post-release was the single most impactful intervention
- Risk scores from the model outperformed traditional assessment tools

## Future Improvements

- Incorporate more granular program participation data
- Explore causal inference methods to better isolate program effects
